## How they met

DanWen and Il met through mutual friends at a dinner in Melbourne's east — a quiet introduction that neither of them thought much of at the time. A few months later they were inseparable. A few years after that, they decided to make it official in a way that felt completely theirs.

## The day

They started the morning with couple portraits at Chanel Melbourne — a nod to the things they love, before the ceremony made everything permanent. From there it was a short drive to Parliament House for pre-ceremony photos on the steps, the kind of Melbourne backdrop that never gets old.

The ceremony itself was at Victoria Marriage Registry: intimate, unhurried, genuinely moving. Il's hands shook slightly when he took DanWen's. Nobody mentioned it but everyone noticed.

Carlton Gardens came next — afternoon light filtering through the old elms, the two of them walking like they had nowhere else to be. Then a long drive east to Wantirna, where both families had gathered for an after party that ran well past anyone's bedtime.

## A moment from the day

> "We didn't want a big traditional wedding. We wanted a day that felt like us. It did."
> — DanWen & Il
