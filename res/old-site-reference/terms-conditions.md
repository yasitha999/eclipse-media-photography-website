# Terms & Conditions — reference notes

> **Source**: Old WordPress site.
> **Status**: Off-the-shelf SaaS boilerplate — **do not reuse as-is**.
> Many clauses are wrong for a wedding photographer
> ("Purchases must provide accurate payment and shipping information",
> subscription / newsletter provisions, "introducing malware",
> DMCA procedure for user-submitted content).
>
> **Reuse plan**: Write a proper photography-specific T&Cs page from
> scratch, covering the actual business terms below. Better: get a
> lawyer / use a wedding-photography template (PPA, AIPP, or similar
> industry body).

---

## What a real wedding-photography T&Cs page should cover

1. **Parties & engagement** — who is contracting with whom, effective date.
2. **Booking & retainer** — amount, non-refundable, what it reserves.
3. **Balance & payment terms** — when the balance is due (usually 2–4 weeks pre-wedding), accepted payment methods, late payment.
4. **Cancellation & postponement** — photographer's and client's rights, retainer forfeiture, rescheduling policy.
5. **Force majeure** — how illness, injury, natural disasters, pandemics are handled. Referenced loosely in the old FAQ; needs formal treatment here.
6. **Deliverables** — number of edited images (range), delivery timeline (8 weeks per FAQ), delivery format (online gallery), print release vs. commercial licence distinction.
7. **Copyright & usage** — photographer retains copyright; client gets a personal-use licence; commercial use requires written permission.
8. **Model release** — photographer's right to use images in portfolio, website, social media, competitions, editorial submissions.
9. **Travel & expenses** — coverage area included; travel fees for destinations outside the included area (per FAQ: flight, lodging, meals at cost).
10. **Backup photographer / emergency** — what happens if the photographer can't attend (per FAQ: trusted network, same style, editing by the original photographer).
11. **Limitation of liability** — capped at contract value; no liability for missed moments due to circumstances beyond reasonable control.
12. **Client obligations** — provide accurate timeline, meal if coverage exceeds 6 hrs, access / permissions at venue, cooperative participation.
13. **Third-party content** — no liability for guest photos, venue lighting, weather.
14. **Dispute resolution & jurisdiction** — Victoria, Australia; attempt mediation first.
15. **Contact** — `info@eclipsemedia.au` for queries.

## Original text (verbatim, for reference only)

*(The old Terms page was generic SaaS boilerplate covering purchases,
subscriptions, contests, newsletter consent, "shipping information",
DMCA procedures for user-submitted infringing content, and "refunds
within 30 days of original purchase" — none of which match how a
wedding photographer actually operates. Discarding in favour of a
proper rewrite.)*

Key clauses worth carrying across:

- **Refunds**: "Refunds for Contracts" issued "within 30 days of the original purchase." *(Needs restatement: retainer is non-refundable once paid; balance is refundable only if photographer cancels.)*
- **Intellectual Property**: "Eclipse Media Photography retains exclusive property rights to the Service and original content." *(Good — keep, reframe for photographic copyright.)*
- **Governing Law**: "These Terms shall be governed in accordance with the laws of Australia." *(Keep, narrow to Victoria.)*
- **Age**: "Service is intended only for access and use by individuals at least eighteen (18) years old." *(Keep.)*
